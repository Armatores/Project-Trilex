## 1. THERMAL CHALLENGE AND SOLUTION

### 1.1. The Risk: Logical Entropy
While the OPU operates on low-power, pulsed laser energy, the non-linear materials in the Trizistor will inevitably absorb a minuscule amount of energy, creating localized heat spikes.
* **Effect:** Heat causes thermal expansion ($\Delta L$), changing the optical path length, which leads to a **Phase Mismatch** ($\Delta \phi$).
* **Consequence:** $\Delta \phi$ forces the Trizistor to output 0 (Silence) even when the vectors are logically matched.

### 1.2. The Defense: Active Phase Correction (APC)
The TIU implements a system of **active compensation** instead of passive cooling.

## 2. TIU ARCHITECTURE

### 2.1. Passive Isolation Layer
* **Hardware:** The Trizistor PIC is mounted on a substrate with an extremely low Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE), such as **Zero-Expansion Quartz** or **Zerodur**.
* **Function:** Minimizes the transfer of localized heat spikes to the larger chip body.

### 2.2. Active Correction Loop (APC)
The APC loop constantly monitors and corrects the phase of the light path.
* **Sensors:** Micro-thermosensors are integrated directly adjacent to the non-linear chamber.
* **Actuators:** Highly localized, low-power **Thermoelectric Micro-Heaters** (powered by the Stabilization Rail $P_3$).
* **Mechanism:**
    1.  The sensor detects a rise in temperature ($\Delta T$).
    2.  The control circuit immediately instructs the micro-heater to generate a counteracting, localized heat pulse.
    3.  This controlled heat pulse induces a **compensatory thermal expansion** in the opposite direction, actively stabilizing the phase of the light path ($\Delta \phi \approx 0$).

### 2.3. Compliance with Efficiency
The system only uses energy (from $P_3$) to create corrective heat pulses, maintaining a high level of overall energy efficiency ($\Sigma K \to \min$) while ensuring **absolute logical fidelity**.
